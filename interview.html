Professional IT Interview

Scott Nailon

Web designer and hosting company owner

15 Years Experience

What kind of work is done by the IT professional?

Scott Runs a web design and hosting company and maintains allied hosting brands. Scott also runs his own servers, keeping them in a local data center in Sydney to provide a computing power resource service 24 hours a day 7 days a week.

What kinds of people does the IT professional interact with? Are they other IT professionals? Clients? Investors? The general public?

Scott deals with marketers, small business owners such as clients, other IT professionals such as Windows support, Industry peers such as other server hosting professionals and experienced Linux users.

It is noted that he usually works individually instead of interacting with others due to time constraints.

Where does the IT professional spend most of their time?

Scott spends most of his time working from a home office workspace. The recent Covid pandemic has not affected Scott's work as he was based from home prior to the pandemic starting.

Previously Scott worked out of the East Coast but quickly found that working from home was more convenient, professionally, and personally.

What aspect of their position is most challenging?

Scott has found time management was his biggest challenge. Some of the challanges include working from home with four other family members present and dealing with other peers and marketers with little to no experience.

Below is a list of several other challanges Scott faces on a regular basis.

  • Time management throughout all aspects of IT
  • Keeping up to date
  • Balance of learning what to learn for current and future business
  • Take breaks and to keep your physical health up instead of working too much
  • Not spending too much money on equipment

Professional IT Career Advice

Scott's advice to anyone looking to get into the IT industry is to learn bussiness management. It will help you understand the fundamentals of running a bussiness even if you don't plan to run your own one day.

Below is a list of several other pieces of advice Scott has for others.

  • Study business management
  • Study and work to your strengths and interests
  • Try to get a broad perspective of all IT fields
